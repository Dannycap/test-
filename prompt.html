Prompting Guide for Investment Finance

1. Clarity & Specificity

  • “Act as an equity analyst with 10 years’ experience.”
  • “List the top 5 ETFs by 5-year annualized return.”

2. Explicit Constraints

  • “Provide a table with columns: Ticker, P/E, Dividend Yield, Expense Ratio.”
  • “Summarize in no more than three bullet points.”

3. Prompt Chaining

  1. “Analyze the revenue growth of AAPL over 5 years.”
  2. “Evaluate its debt-to-equity trend.”
  3. “Based on these, suggest potential risks.”

4. Domain-Specific Adaptations

  • “Using this CSV of 10-year treasury yields, show yield curve changes.”
  • “Compare valuations using DCF assumptions.”

5. Template & Pattern Usage

  • Persona Pattern: “You are a risk manager; identify top portfolio risks.”
  • Template Pattern: “Report format: • Summary • Metrics • Recommendation.”

Good Prompt Examples

  • “Act as a veteran equity analyst and summarize the financial health of TSLA for the past 3 years, focusing on liquidity and profitability ratios.”
  • “Provide a side-by-side comparison table of VOO vs. IVV including expense ratios, average annual returns, and top 3 sector exposures.”
  • “Simulate a risk report: If interest rates rise by 2%, what is the likely impact on long-duration corporate bond ETFs?”
  • “Draft an investment thesis outline for Bitcoin, covering macro catalysts, technical trends, and major risks, in three bullet points each.”
  • “Compare and contrast covered call vs. protective put strategies on AAPL stock, listing pros, cons, and breakeven points.”
